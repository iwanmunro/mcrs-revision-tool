# MRCS Part A Syllabus Reference

Source: Medibuddy / Royal College of Surgeons MRCS Part A syllabus

## Exam Structure

The MRCS Part A is a **five-hour multiple-choice exam** consisting of two papers sat on the same day.

| Paper | Time | Duration | Questions |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| Paper 1 – Applied Basic Science | AM | 3 hours (two 90-min sections, 10-min break) | 180 SBA questions |
| Paper 2 – Principles of Surgery in General | PM | 2 hours | 120 SBA questions |

All questions use **Single Best Answer (SBA)** format: five options, one correct answer.

---

## The 10 MRCS Part A Syllabus Modules

1. Basic science knowledge relevant to surgical practice
2. Common surgical conditions
3. Basic surgical skills
4. The assessment and management of the surgical patient
5. Perioperative care of the surgical patient
6. Assessment and early treatment of the patient with trauma
7. Surgical care of the paediatric patient
8. Management of the dying patient
9. Organ and tissue transplantation
10. Professional behaviour and leadership skills

## Paper 1 – Applied Basic Science (180 questions)

### 1. Applied Surgical Anatomy (75 questions)

- Mediastinal divisions: superior, anterior, middle, posterior
- Lungs: lobes, fissures, bronchopulmonary segments, roots
- Heart: chambers, valves, coronary circulation, cardiac conduction

- Thoracic duct, azygos system
- Phrenic nerve (C3,4,5), vagus nerve, sympathetic chain
- Thoracic inlet and outlet; rib anatomy; sternum

**Abdomen (15 questions)**
- Anterior abdominal wall: layers, Hesselbach's triangle, inguinal canal
- Inguinal ligament, lacunar ligament, femoral canal
- Peritoneum: reflections, lesser sac, omental bursa
- Oesophagus and stomach: blood supply, lymphatics, relations
- Small intestine: duodenum (parts, relations, blood supply); jejunum and ileum
- Large intestine: caecum, appendix position, colon blood supply, rectum and anal canal
- Liver: lobes (Couinaud segments), portal triad, hepatic veins, bile ducts
- Gallbladder and biliary tree: calot's triangle, cystic artery
- Pancreas: head, body, tail, main duct, blood supply; islets of Langerhans
- Spleen: notched superior border, ligaments, blood supply
- Kidneys: relations, hilum contents (VUA), renal fascia, para-aortic nodes
- Ureters: course, constrictions, blood supply
- Adrenal glands: arterial supply (triple supply), venous drainage
- Aorta and IVC: branches, bifurcation levels
- Coeliac axis, SMA, IMA territories; portal venous system
- Retroperitoneum and lumbar plexus

**Pelvis (4 questions)**
- Pelvic inlet, outlet, walls and floor
- Pelvic organs: bladder, uterus, ovaries, prostate, seminal vesicles, rectum
- Pelvic fascia and spaces
- Pelvic splanchnic nerves; hypogastric plexus
- Obturator nerve, femoral nerve, sciatic nerve in pelvis
- Pelvic lymphatics; iliac vessels

**Perineum (2 questions)**
- Anatomy of ischiorectal fossa; anal sphincters; anal canal lining and blood supply
- Male and female perineum; pudendal nerve; perineal body

**Upper Limb (part of Limbs – 15 questions total)**
- Brachial plexus: roots (C5-T1), trunks, divisions, cords, terminal branches
- Axillary artery and its branches
- Deltoid, rotator cuff muscles and their nerve supply
- Cubital fossa contents
- Radial, ulnar, median nerve courses and motor/sensory territories
- Carpal tunnel: contents and boundaries

- Thenar and hypothenar muscle innervation

**Lower Limb (part of Limbs – 15 questions total)**
- Femoral triangle: boundaries and contents (NAVY mnemonic)
- Femoral nerve, femoral artery, femoral vein, femoral canal
- Adductor canal (Hunter's canal)
- Popliteal fossa: boundaries and contents
- Knee joint: cruciate ligaments, collateral ligaments, menisci, blood supply
- Sciatic nerve: divisions into common peroneal and tibial
- Common peroneal nerve: lateral neck of fibula, deep and superficial divisions
- Foot: plantar fascia, sole layers, Achilles tendon and blood supply
- Lumbosacral plexus contributions to lower limb nerve supply

**Spine (3 questions)**
- Vertebral levels and clinical landmarks: T4 (sternal angle), T10 (umbilicus), L4 (iliac crests)
- Intervertebral disc anatomy; nucleus pulposus and annulus fibrosus
- Spinal cord termination (L1/2 in adults); cauda equina
- Dermatomes and myotomes for key nerve roots (C5-T1 upper limb; L2-S2 lower limb)
- Epidural and spinal anaesthesia anatomy

**Head and Neck (10 questions)**
- Parotid gland: relationships, facial nerve (VII) branches (TMPZM)
- Submandibular gland: lingual nerve, hypoglossal nerve relations
- Thyroid gland: blood supply, RLN and external laryngeal nerve relations
- Parathyroid glands: superior and inferior; embryological origin
- Neck triangles: anterior and posterior; contents
- Carotid sheath: common carotid, internal jugular vein, vagus nerve
- Cervical lymph nodes: levels I–VI
- Larynx: cartilages, vocal cords, innervation; cricothyroid membrane (emergency airway)
- Cranial nerves: course and clinical testing relevant to surgery
- Parapharyngeal and retropharyngeal spaces
- Mandibular nerve and infratemporal fossa

**Brain (6 questions)**
- Cerebral lobes and functional areas
- Circle of Willis: internal carotid system, vertebrobasilar system
- Ventricular system and CSF circulation
- Meninges: dura mater, arachnoid, pia mater; epidural vs subdural vs subarachnoid space
- Cranial nerve exits from skull: foramina
- Cerebellum: connections; presentation of cerebellar lesions
- Spinal cord cross-section: anterior horn, posterior columns, spinothalamic tracts

**Autonomic Nervous System (2 questions)**
- Sympathetic: thoracolumbar outflow; paravertebral and prevertebral ganglia
- Parasympathetic: craniosacral outflow; ganglia and target organs
- Sympathetic supply to head (superior cervical ganglion → Horner's syndrome)
- Enteric nervous system; visceral pain pathways

#### Surgically Related Embryology and Development (8 questions)
- At least 1 question each from: Thorax, Perineum, Head & Neck
- Development of the gut: foregut/midgut/hindgut derivatives
- Tracheo-oesophageal fistula; oesophageal atresia
- Branchial arches 1–6: skeletal, muscular, nerve, artery derivatives
- Branchial cysts, sinuses and fistulae
- Thyroglossal duct and cysts; pyramidal lobe
- Developmental abnormalities of the kidney: horseshoe, pelvic kidney, duplex
- Hypospadias, epispadias; undescended testis
- Patent urachus; Meckel's diverticulum (rule of 2s)
- Congenital diaphragmatic hernia (Bochdalek)
- Development of the midgut: malrotation, volvulus

#### Surface and Imaging Anatomy (4 questions)
- At least 1 from head & neck
- Plain film interpretation: chest X-ray (PA), abdominal X-ray, orthopaedic films
- CT cross-sectional anatomy: key levels (T2, T4, L1, L4)
- Ultrasound-guided procedures: hepatic, renal, groin
- Surface landmarks for procedures: thoracocentesis, lumbar puncture, joint aspiration

### 2. Applied Surgical Physiology (45 questions)

#### General Physiological Principles (15 questions)
- Fluid compartments: ICF, ECF (interstitial + plasma); Starling forces
- Acid-base balance: Henderson-Hasselbalch; metabolic and respiratory acidosis/alkalosis; anion gap
- Blood gas interpretation; oxyhaemoglobin dissociation curve (Bohr effect)
- Electrolytes: sodium, potassium, chloride, bicarbonate; hypo/hypernatraemia
- Osmolality; SIADH; diabetes insipidus
- Renal handling of sodium and water: aldosterone, ADH, ANP
- Starling's law of the heart; Frank-Starling mechanism; cardiac output = HR × SV
- Systemic vascular resistance; mean arterial pressure
- Oxygen delivery (DO2) and consumption (VO2); oxygen debt
- Temperature regulation and hypothermia
- Nutrition: BMR, Harris-Benedict; catabolism and anabolism; nitrogen balance
- Metabolic response to surgery: neuroendocrine, cytokine; SIRS/CARS
- Blood groups: ABO, Rhesus; cross-matching; transfusion reactions
- Coagulation cascade: intrinsic, extrinsic, common pathways; fibrinolysis
- Haemostasis: primary (platelet plug) and secondary (coagulation)

#### Physiology of Specific Organ Systems (30 questions, 5 per system)

**Cardiovascular System (5 questions)**
- Action potential: phases 0–4; pacemaker vs non-pacemaker cells; SA node automaticity
- Cardiac cycle: systole/diastole; pressure-volume loops; Wiggers diagram
- Coronary blood flow: autoregulation; myocardial oxygen demand
- Peripheral circulation; arteriovenous difference; capillary exchange
- Regulation of blood pressure: baroreceptor reflex; carotid sinus; aortic arch

**Respiratory System (5 questions)**
- Lung volumes and capacities: TV, IRV, ERV, RV, FRC, TLC, VC
- Dead space: anatomical vs physiological; V/Q matching
- Lung mechanics: compliance; surfactant; Laplace's law; work of breathing
- Gas exchange: Fick's law; diffusion limitation vs perfusion limitation
- Control of breathing: central and peripheral chemoreceptors; hypoxic drive
- Pulmonary circulation: low-pressure; hypoxic vasoconstriction

**Gastrointestinal System (5 questions)**
- Salivary and gastric secretion: composition; phases (cephalic, gastric, intestinal)
- Gastric acid: parietal cells; proton pump; intrinsic factor; pepsinogen
- Pancreatic exocrine secretion: bicarbonate; enzymes; secretin and CCK
- Bile: composition; enterohepatic circulation; cholestasis
- Small intestinal absorption: Na-glucose co-transport; fat digestion (micelles)
- Colonic function: water absorption; bacterial flora; short-chain fatty acids
- GI motility: migrating myoelectric complex; gastric emptying; ileocaecal valve

**Urinary System (5 questions)**
- GFR: filtered fraction; Starling forces; autoregulation; creatinine clearance
- Tubular function: proximal reabsorption (67% NaCl, H2O, HCO3, glucose, amino acids)
- Loop of Henle: counter-current multiplier
- Distal tubule and collecting duct: aldosterone; ADH (vasopressin)
- Renal handling of potassium and hydrogen ions
- Urinary concentration and dilution; osmotic gradient in medulla
- Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS)

**Endocrine System (5 questions)**
- Hypothalamic-pituitary axis: releasing hormones, trophic hormones, feedback
- Thyroid hormones: T3, T4; synthesis; peripheral conversion; effects on BMR
- Adrenal cortex: glucocorticoids (cortisol), mineralocorticoids (aldosterone), androgens
- Adrenal medulla: catecholamines; fight-or-flight response
- Insulin and glucagon: fed/fasting state; glycaemic control; counter-regulatory hormones
- Calcium homeostasis: PTH, calcitonin, vitamin D; bone turnover
- Stress response to surgery: cortisol, catecholamines, ADH, GH, glucagon surge

**Neurological System (5 questions)**
- Resting membrane potential; action potential propagation; saltatory conduction
- Neuromuscular junction: acetylcholine, acetylcholinesterase; depolarising/non-depolarising NMBAs
- Cerebral blood flow: autoregulation; CO2 reactivity; Monroe-Kellie doctrine; ICP
- CSF production and absorption; hydrocephalus
- Spinal cord tracts: dorsal column (proprioception, vibration); spinothalamic (pain, temperature)
- Nociception: transduction, transmission, modulation, perception; central sensitisation

### 3. Applied Surgical Pathology (37 questions)

#### General Pathological Principles (9 questions)
- Inflammation: acute (vascular, cellular phases; mediators: histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, cytokines) and chronic (granulomatous; TB, sarcoid, Crohn's)
- Cell injury: reversible vs irreversible; apoptosis vs necrosis (coagulative, liquefactive, caseous, fat)
- Wound healing: primary vs secondary intention; phases (haemostasis, inflammation, proliferation, remodelling); factors impairing healing
- Repair and regeneration: labile, stable, permanent cells; stem cells
- Oedema: causes (↑ hydrostatic pressure, ↓ oncotic pressure, lymphatic obstruction, ↑ capillary permeability)
- Thrombosis: Virchow's triad (stasis, endothelial damage, hypercoagulability); thrombus vs clot
- Embolism: pulmonary, systemic, fat, air, amniotic fluid
- Hypertrophy, hyperplasia, atrophy, metaplasia, dysplasia

#### Surgical Immunology (2 questions)
- Innate immunity: neutrophils, macrophages, natural killer cells; pattern recognition receptors
- Adaptive immunity: B cells, T cells; MHC I and MHC II; antigen presentation
- Antibody classes: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, IgD; complement
- Hypersensitivity: Types I–IV; latex allergy; anaphylaxis management
- Transplant rejection: hyperacute, acute, chronic; immunosuppression principles

#### Surgical Haematology (2 questions)
- Anaemia: classification (microcytic, normocytic, macrocytic); iron deficiency vs B12/folate
- Sickle cell disease and thalassaemia: surgical implications; sickling crisis prevention
- Polycythaemia; leukaemias in surgical context
- Platelet disorders: thrombocytopaenia; ITP; TTP
- Coagulopathies: haemophilia A and B; von Willebrand disease; DIC
- Anticoagulants and reversal: heparin (protamine), warfarin (vitamin K, FFP), DOACs

#### Surgical Clinical Chemistry (2 questions)
- Liver function tests: bilirubin, ALP, ALT, AST, GGT, albumin, PT
- Renal function tests: urea, creatinine, eGFR, electrolytes; AKI staging (KDIGO)
- Tumour markers: CEA (colorectal), AFP (hepatocellular/germ cell), PSA, CA-125, CA 19-9
- Amylase and lipase: pancreatitis diagnosis; non-pancreatic causes of raised amylase

#### Principles of Neoplasia and Oncology (2 questions)
- Oncogenes (proto-oncogenes, RAS, MYC), tumour suppressor genes (TP53, RB, APC, BRCA)
- Carcinogenesis: initiation, promotion, progression; environmental carcinogens
- TNM staging; grading vs staging; curative vs palliative intent
- Principles of chemotherapy: cell cycle; antimetabolites, alkylating agents, vinca alkaloids
- Radiotherapy: principles; fractionation; complications
- Paraneoplastic syndromes: SIADH, ectopic ACTH, hypercalcaemia of malignancy (PTHrP)

#### Organ System Pathology (20 questions, 2 per system)

**Cardiovascular System** – Atherosclerosis pathogenesis; myocardial infarction: morphology, complications; aneurysm types; aortic dissection

**Respiratory System** – Pneumonia: lobar vs broncho; ARDS: diffuse alveolar damage; lung cancer: squamous, adenocarcinoma, SCLC, mesothelioma; asthma and COPD pathology

**Digestive System** – Appendicitis: acute inflammation; colorectal cancer: polyp → carcinoma sequence; oesophageal cancer: squamous vs adenocarcinoma; Barrett's oesophagus; Crohn's vs ulcerative colitis: key histological differences; peptic ulcer disease; gastric cancer

**Genitourinary System** – Renal cell carcinoma (clear cell): von Hippel-Lindau; bladder cancer: transitional cell; prostate cancer: Gleason grading; renal calculi: types and composition; testicular cancer: seminoma vs non-seminoma

**Central and Peripheral Neurological Systems** – Glioblastoma multiforme; meningioma; acoustic neuroma; peripheral neuropathy in surgery; neuropraxia, axonotmesis, neurotmesis (Seddon classification)

**Skin Cancer** – Basal cell carcinoma: nodular, superficial, morphoeic; squamous cell carcinoma: risk factors, perineural invasion; malignant melanoma: Breslow thickness, Clark level, ABCDE criteria, sentinel node biopsy; Marjolin's ulcer

**Lymphoreticular System** – Hodgkin lymphoma: Reed-Sternberg cells; non-Hodgkin lymphoma classification; splenomegaly: causes; hyposplenism consequences

**Musculoskeletal System** – Osteosarcoma, Ewing's sarcoma; chondrosarcoma; bone metastases (osteolytic vs osteosclerotic); osteoarthritis vs rheumatoid arthritis pathology; pathological fractures

**Pathology of the Breast** – Fibrocystic change; fibroadenoma; phyllodes tumour; ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS); invasive ductal carcinoma; invasive lobular carcinoma; Paget's disease of nipple; breast cancer grading (Elston-Ellis) and staging

**Pathology of the Endocrine Glands** – Thyroid: goitre, Hashimoto's, Graves', papillary carcinoma, follicular carcinoma, medullary carcinoma (RET mutation, MEN2), anaplastic; parathyroid adenoma; phaeochromocytoma; MEN1, MEN2A, MEN2B; Conn's syndrome; Cushing's

### 4. Pharmacology as Applied to Surgical Practice (8 questions)

- **Analgesics**: Paracetamol; NSAID mechanism/COX inhibition; opioid pharmacology (morphine, fentanyl, codeine, tramadol); opioid receptors (mu, kappa, delta); naloxone
- **Antibiotics**: Penicillins (beta-lactam mechanism); cephalosporins; carbapenems; vancomycin (MRSA); metronidazole (anaerobes); flucloxacillin; gentamicin (aminoglycoside toxicity); prophylaxis principles
- **Anaesthetic agents**: Thiopental; propofol; ketamine; suxamethonium (depolarising NMBA, pseudocholinesterase); rocuronium, atracurium (non-depolarising); sevoflurane; nitrous oxide; MAC concept
- **Local anaesthetics**: Mechanism (Na+ channel block); amide (lidocaine, bupivacaine) vs ester; maximum doses; toxic dose; LAST (local anaesthetic systemic toxicity)
- **Anticoagulants and antiplatelets**: Heparin (UFH, LMWH); warfarin (mechanism, monitoring, reversal); DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran); aspirin, clopidogrel; bridging therapy
- **Cardiovascular drugs**: Beta-blockers; ACE inhibitors; calcium channel blockers; digoxin; amiodarone; vasopressors (noradrenaline, adrenaline, dopamine, vasopressin)
- **Fluid therapy**: Crystalloids (normal saline, Hartmann's, 5% dextrose); colloids; plasma-Lyte; hyperchloraemic acidosis
- **Chemotherapy agents**: Drug classes and key toxicities; 5-FU, oxaliplatin, capecitabine

### 5. Microbiology as Applied to Surgical Practice (7 questions)

- Gram-positive cocci: Staphylococcus aureus (toxins, MRSA), Streptococcus pyogenes (necrotising fasciitis), Streptococcus pneumoniae
- Gram-negative rods: E. coli, Klebsiella, Pseudomonas aeruginosa (immunocompromised), Haemophilus influenzae
- Anaerobes: Bacteroides fragilis; Clostridium difficile; Clostridium perfringens (gas gangrene); tetanus
- Surgical site infection (SSI): classification (superficial, deep, organ/space); risk factors; prevention (antibiotic prophylaxis, skin prep, normothermia)
- Sepsis and septic shock: definition (Sepsis-3); SOFA score; empirical antibiotics; source control
- Necrotising fasciitis: Type I (polymicrobial) and Type II (Group A Strep); Fournier's gangrene; management (wide debridement)
- Hospital-acquired infections: MRSA decolonisation; VRE; Acinetobacter; carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae
- C. difficile: pathophysiology; pseudomembranous colitis; treatment (oral vancomycin, fidaxomicin); infection control
- Prion diseases: CJD; precautions for contaminated instruments
- Antifungals: Candida; azoles; amphotericin B

### 6. Imaging as Applied to Surgical Practice (5 questions)

- CT imaging: Hounsfield units; windows (lung, bone, soft tissue, liver); contrast phases (arterial, portal venous, delayed)
- Plain chest X-ray: systematic interpretation (ABCDE); pneumothorax; haemothorax; surgical emphysema; pleural effusion
- Abdominal X-ray: bowel gas patterns; air under diaphragm (perforation); calcification (gallstones, renal, AAA)
- MRI: principles; indications in surgery (rectal MRI for staging, liver lesion characterisation, MSK)
- Ultrasound: FAST scan; hepatobiliary; thyroid; venous Doppler; EBUS/EUS
- Nuclear medicine: bone scan; HIDA; PET-CT for staging

### 7. Data Interpretation and Audit (3 questions)

- Statistical terms: sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV, likelihood ratios
- Types of study design: RCT, cohort, case-control, cross-sectional; hierarchy of evidence; systematic review and meta-analysis
- Audit cycle vs research vs quality improvement; NICE, SIGN guidelines
- Survival analysis: Kaplan-Meier curves; hazard ratio; log-rank test
- Risk: absolute risk, relative risk, number needed to treat (NNT), odds ratio
- Bias and confounding; intention-to-treat vs per-protocol analysis

## Paper 2 – Principles of Surgery in General (120 questions)

### 1. Common Congenital and Acquired Surgical Conditions (45 questions)

**Gastrointestinal Disease (7 questions)**
- Colorectal cancer: staging, Lynch syndrome, FAP, surveillance colonoscopy; Dukes vs TNM
- Inflammatory bowel disease: Crohn's vs UC (clinical, endoscopic, histological differences); surgical indications; ileal pouch
- Appendicitis: Alvarado score; Rovsing's sign; perforation risk; laparoscopic appendicectomy
- Cholecystitis and cholelithiasis: bile composition; Hartmann's pouch; common bile duct stones; ERCP; Mirizzi syndrome
- Pancreatitis: Ranson/Glasgow/APACHE scoring; Atlanta classification; ERCP complications; IPMN; pseudocyst
- Hernia: inguinal (direct vs indirect), femoral, umbilical, incisional, Spigelian; Littre's hernia (Meckel's in sac); Richter's hernia; incarceration vs strangulation
- Diverticular disease: Hinchey classification; Hartmann's procedure; Sigmoid colectomy

**Breast Disease (3 questions)**
- Triple assessment: clinical, imaging (mammogram, USS), biopsy (FNA vs core)
- Breast cancer management: BCS vs mastectomy; sentinel lymph node biopsy; SLNB; axillary clearance
- Adjuvant therapy: endocrine (tamoxifen, aromatase inhibitors), chemotherapy, herceptin (trastuzumab); radiotherapy indications
- Benign breast disease: fibroadenoma, phyllodes, fibrocystic disease, duct ectasia, periductal mastitis, Paget's, nipple discharge

**Vascular Disease (4 questions)**
- Peripheral arterial disease: ABPI; Fontaine classification (I–IV); claudication vs critical limb ischaemia
- Acute limb ischaemia: six Ps; embolus vs thrombosis; Fogarty catheter; 4-hour rule
- Aortic aneurysm: definition (>3cm AAA); rupture risk; EVAR vs open; endoleak types (I–V)
- Carotid artery disease: symptomatic vs asymptomatic; carotid endarterectomy; NASCET trial; CEA vs stenting
- Varicose veins: CEAP classification; great saphenous vein; incompetent perforators; EVLA, RFA, foam sclerotherapy
- DVT and PE: Wells score; LMWH; IVC filter indications; catheter-directed thrombolysis

**Cardiovascular and Pulmonary Disease (4 questions)**
- Ischaemic heart disease and surgical risk: Goldman cardiac risk index; revised Lee index; METS
- Valvular heart disease: aortic stenosis (critical gradient), mitral regurgitation – perioperative implications
- Lung cancer: staging (TNM); resectability criteria; FEV1 predicted post-op; mediastinoscopy; VATS
- Pneumothorax: primary vs secondary spontaneous; tension pneumothorax management; BTS guidelines; pleurodesis
- Pleural effusion: Light's criteria (exudate vs transudate); empyema; pleural biopsy indications

**Genitourinary Disease (4 questions)**
- Renal calculi: types (calcium oxalate 80%, uric acid, struvite, cystine); ESWL; ureteroscopy; PCNL; metabolic workup
- Prostate: benign prostatic hyperplasia (IPSS score; TURP; 5-alpha reductase inhibitors); prostate cancer (PSA; Gleason; prostatectomy vs radiotherapy vs watchful waiting)
- Bladder cancer: transitional cell; muscle-invasive vs superficial; TURBT; BCG; radical cystectomy
- Testicular cancer: seminoma vs NSGCT; AFP, beta-hCG, LDH; orchidectomy; retroperitoneal lymph node dissection
- Hydronephrosis; ureteric obstruction; urinary incontinence types

**Orthopaedic Conditions (7 questions)**
- Hip fractures: Garden classification; intracapsular vs extracapsular; DHS vs hemiarthroplasty vs THA
- Compartment syndrome: acute vs chronic; Whitesides technique; fasciotomy; 6Ps; fingertip test
- Osteomyelitis: acute vs chronic; haematogenous vs post-traumatic; Staphylococcus aureus; MRI diagnosis; treatment
- Septic arthritis: hot joint; Kocher criteria; urgent joint washout; late sequelae (AVN)
- Spinal conditions: disc herniation; cauda equina syndrome (red flag, emergency); spinal stenosis; spondylolisthesis
- Bone tumours: primary (osteosarcoma, Ewing's, chondrosarcoma) vs metastatic (breast, lung, prostate, thyroid, kidney)
- Limb arthroplasty: indications, complications (dislocation, periprosthetic fracture, infection); Charnley vs posterior approach

**Diseases of the Skin, Head and Neck (4 questions)**
- Thyroid disease: goitre; thyrotoxicosis (Graves', toxic nodule); hypothyroidism; thyroid nodule assessment (FNA, USS, FNAC); thyroid cancer types; total thyroidectomy; RLN injury; hypoparathyroidism
- Parathyroid disease: primary hyperparathyroidism (adenoma 80%); hypercalcaemia symptoms; technetium sestamibi scan; minimally invasive parathyroidectomy
- Head and neck cancer: squamous cell carcinoma of oral cavity/pharynx/larynx; HPV-positive oropharyngeal cancer; neck dissection (levels I–V, selective vs modified radical); tracheostomy
- Skin lesions: lipoma, epidermal cyst, sebaceous cyst, pilonidal sinus, hidradenitis suppurativa; basal cell carcinoma (rodent ulcer); SCC; melanoma
- Salivary gland disease: pleomorphic adenoma (parotid); Warthin's tumour; parotidectomy; Frey syndrome

**Neurology and Neurosurgery (2 questions)**
- Head injury: GCS; NICE CT head criteria; extradural vs subdural haematoma; diffuse axonal injury
- Brain tumours: glioblastoma, meningioma (en plaque), acoustic neuroma; raised ICP management
- Subarachnoid haemorrhage: thunderclap headache; CT (hyperdense cisterns); LP (xanthochromia); aneurysm clipping vs coiling
- Carpal tunnel syndrome; ulnar nerve entrapment (cubital tunnel); tarsal tunnel

**Endocrine Disease (4 questions)**
- Adrenal disease: Conn's syndrome (hyperaldosteronism); Cushing's (cortisol excess); phaeochromocytoma (catecholamine excess; surgical preparation with alpha then beta blockade); adrenal incidentaloma
- MEN syndromes: MEN1 (pituitary, parathyroid, pancreas); MEN2A (medullary thyroid, phaeochromocytoma, parathyroid); MEN2B (MTC, phaeo, mucosal neuromas)
- Carcinoid tumours: 5-HIAA; carcinoid syndrome (flushing, diarrhoea, bronchospasm); octreotide
- Pancreatic endocrine tumours: insulinoma (Whipple's triad); VIPoma (WDHA/Verner-Morrison); gastrinoma (Zollinger-Ellison)

**Lymphoreticular System (2 questions)**
- Lymphoma staging: Ann Arbor I–IV; B symptoms
- Splenectomy: indications; vaccination protocol (pneumococcus, meningococcus, Haemophilus influenzae B); overwhelming post-splenectomy infection (OPSI)
- Lymphoedema: primary vs secondary; Stewart-Treves syndrome (angiosarcoma in lymphoedematous arm)

**Cancer Therapy and Palliative Care (2 questions)**
- Principles of multimodal cancer treatment: surgery + chemotherapy + radiotherapy + targeted therapy
- Palliative intent: quality of life; symptom control; opioid analgesic ladder (WHO); anticipatory prescribing
- Pain management: adjuvants (gabapentin, amitriptyline, steroids); syringe driver; palliative radiotherapy

**Acute Emergencies (2 questions)**
- Acute abdomen: systematic approach; bowel obstruction (SBO vs LBO, strangulation); volvulus; abdominal aortic aneurysm rupture
- Haemorrhage control: damage control resuscitation; massive transfusion protocol (1:1:1 ratio); permissive hypotension

### 2. Perioperative Management (35 questions)

**Preoperative Assessment and Management (7 questions)**
- ASA classification: I–V (definitions and examples)
- Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPEX): VO2max; anaerobic threshold; risk stratification
- DVT/PE prophylaxis: Caprini/Rogers score; LMWH; TED stockings; IVC filter; timing post-surgery
- Antibiotic prophylaxis: timing (within 60 min); choice by procedure; repeat doses; NICE/SIGN guidelines
- Blood transfusion: crossmatch; type and screen; maximum surgical blood order schedule (MSBOS); cell salvage; jehovah's witnesses
- Management of anticoagulation and antiplatelet agents perioperatively
- Liver disease and surgical risk: Child-Pugh score; MELD score; coagulopathy; encephalopathy; ascites

**Intraoperative Care (5 questions)**
- Pneumoperitoneum and laparoscopy physiology: Trendelenburg position; CO2 absorption; trocar injuries
- Diathermy/electrosurgery: monopolar vs bipolar; cutting vs coagulation; arcing; burns; pacemakers
- Nerve monitoring: recurrent laryngeal nerve (thyroid surgery); facial nerve (parotid)
- Tourniquets: ischaemic time limit; inflation pressure; complications; exsanguination (Esmarch)
- Sutures and needles: absorbable (Vicryl, PDS) vs non-absorbable (Prolene, Nylon); monofilament vs braided; needle types

**Perioperative Care (2 questions)**
- Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS): principles; pre-operative carbohydrate loading; early mobilisation; multimodal analgesia
- Laparoscopic vs open surgery: physiological advantages; conversion rates; port-site hernia

**Postoperative Care (8 questions)**
- Postoperative complications: immediate (<24h), early (1–7 days), late (>1 week) — with examples
- Respiratory complications: atelectasis; postoperative pneumonia; pulmonary embolism; ARDS
- Cardiovascular complications: postoperative MI; arrhythmias; cardiac failure
- Renal complications: AKI — prerenal, renal, postrenal; contrast nephropathy; urinary retention
- Wound complications: seroma, haematoma, dehiscence, fistula, SSI (CDC classification)
- Anastomotic leak: risk factors; clinical signs; CT with contrast; management
- Ileus vs mechanical obstruction: clinical and radiological differentiation; conservative management
- Fluid balance: insensible losses; third-space losses; hourly urine output targets (>0.5ml/kg/h)

**Nutritional Management (2 questions)**
- Nutritional assessment: MUST score; BMI; albumin; pre-albumin
- Enteral vs parenteral nutrition: preferred route; nasogastric vs nasojejunal; TPN indications and complications (line sepsis, hyperglycaemia, electrolyte disturbance, refeeding syndrome)
- Refeeding syndrome: phosphate, magnesium, potassium; thiamine supplementation
- Immunonutrition: arginine, glutamine, omega-3 fatty acids

**Haemostasis and Blood Products (3 questions)**
- Blood components: packed red cells, platelets, fresh frozen plasma, cryoprecipitate, albumin; specific indications
- Massive haemorrhage: >10 units PRBCs in 24h; massive transfusion protocol; 1:1:1 ratio (PRBC:FFP:platelets)
- Transfusion reactions: febrile non-haemolytic, acute haemolytic, TRALI, TACO, bacterial contamination; management
- Cell salvage contraindications; autologous blood donation

**Coagulation, DVT and Embolism (3 questions)**
- VTE prophylaxis: pharmacological (LMWH) and mechanical (IPD, TED); timing; high risk situations (orthopaedics, malignancy, prolonged surgery)
- Pulmonary embolism: Wells score; CTPA; echocardiography; thrombolysis vs thrombectomy criteria; PESI score
- Inherited thrombophilias: factor V Leiden, prothrombin gene mutation, protein C/S deficiency, antithrombin III deficiency, antiphospholipid syndrome
- Perioperative management of anticoagulation: bridging therapy; reversal

**Metabolic and Endocrine Disorders (5 questions)**
- Diabetic management perioperatively: VRIII (variable rate IV insulin infusion); glucose targets; wound healing
- Thyrotoxic storm: precipitants; management (propylthiouracil, propranolol, Lugol's iodine, steroids, potassium iodide)
- Adrenal insufficiency: surgical stress; hydrocortisone cover for patients on steroids
- Electrolyte disturbances: hyponatraemia (overcorrection → central pontine myelinolysis); hypokalaemia (arrhythmia risk); hypercalcaemia; hypomagnesaemia

### 3. Assessment and Management of Patients with Trauma (30 questions)

**General Trauma Principles (4 questions)**
- ATLS primary survey: ABCDE; simultaneous assessment and resuscitation
- Secondary survey: head-to-toe examination; AMPLE history
- Mechanism of injury: blunt vs penetrating; high vs low velocity; energy transfer
- Damage control surgery: haemorrhage control → ICU stabilisation → definitive repair

**Shock (2 questions)**
- ATLS haemorrhagic shock classification: Class I–IV (blood volume, HR, BP, RR, urine output, GCS)
- Types of shock: hypovolaemic, distributive (septic, neurogenic, anaphylactic), cardiogenic, obstructive (tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade)
- Fluid resuscitation: permissive hypotension in penetrating trauma; crystalloid vs blood products; massive transfusion activation triggers

**Wounds and Soft Tissue Injuries (4 questions)**
- Wound classification: clean, clean-contaminated, contaminated, dirty/infected; SSI risk
- Traumatic wound management: debridement principles; delayed primary closure; secondary intention
- Blast injuries: primary (pressure wave), secondary (fragment), tertiary (displacement), quaternary (burns, crush)
- Degloving injuries; ring avulsion; amputations: replantation criteria

**Burns (2 questions)**
- Rule of Nines (Wallace); Lund and Browder chart — more accurate
- Burn depth: superficial epidermal (erythema), superficial partial thickness (blisters), deep partial thickness, full thickness (insensate, leathery)
- Parkland formula: 4ml/kg/% burn TBSA crystalloid in first 24h (half in first 8h from injury)
- Inhalation injury signs; cyanide poisoning (from house fires); early intubation indications
- Escharotomy indications; circumferential burns

**Fractures and Dislocations (8 questions)**
- Fracture classification: open vs closed; Gustilo-Anderson (open fractures I–IIIC)
- AO classification principles; stability; Salter-Harris growth plate injuries (I–V)
- Femoral fractures: shaft (traction, blood loss up to 1.5L); neck (Garden); subcapital
- Tibial fractures: plateau (Schatzker); shaft; pilon
- Spinal injury: ASIA classification; spinal cord injury vs cord concussion; neurogenic shock
- Shoulder: ATLS approach; Hill-Sachs; Bankart; anterior dislocation management
- Elbow: supracondylar fracture (children; radial nerve, anterior interosseous nerve); Monteggia/Galeazzi
- Wrist: scaphoid fracture (snuffbox tenderness; AVN risk; need for CT/MRI if X-ray negative)
- Distal radius fractures: Colles', Smith's, Barton's

**Organ-Specific Trauma (10 questions)**
- Head trauma: primary vs secondary brain injury; Monroe-Kellie; Cushing's triad; NICE CT head criteria (Level 1/2); extradural (arterial, lens-shaped) vs subdural haematoma (venous, crescent)
- Chest trauma: tension pneumothorax (needle decompression → chest drain); haemothorax (>1500ml); flail chest; traumatic aortic injury (widened mediastinum); cardiac tamponade (Beck's triad)
- Abdominal trauma: FAST scan; DPL indications; solid organ injuries (liver, spleen — non-operative management in haemodynamically stable patients); bowel injury (delayed diagnosis); diaphragmatic injury
- Pelvic trauma: open book (external rotation force); vertical shear; complex patterns; pelvic binder; external fixator; angioembolisation
- Urological trauma: bladder rupture (intraperitoneal vs extraperitoneal); urethral injury (do not catheterise if suspected); renal trauma (AAST grading)
- Vascular trauma: zone injury; fasciotomy indications; temporary vascular shunts

### 4. Surgical Care of Children (7 questions)

- Paediatric fluid management: Holliday-Segar formula; maintenance fluid calculation; weight-based dosing
- Neonatal surgical emergencies: oesophageal atresia (Vacterl association); congenital diaphragmatic hernia; gastroschisis vs omphalocele; Hirschsprung's disease (absent ganglion cells); intussusception (lead point; USS diagnosis; pneumatic reduction); malrotation with volvulus
- Pyloric stenosis: hypochloraemic metabolic alkalosis; projectile vomiting; olive-shaped mass; Ramstedt pyloromyotomy
- Paediatric trauma: AMPLE; child protection; non-accidental injury (metaphyseal fractures, rib fractures, skull fractures; retinal haemorrhage; bruising in non-mobile children)
- Undescended testis: orchidopexy; risk of malignancy; fertility; orchidopexy timing (<18 months)
- Paediatric hernias: inguinal hernia (higher risk of incarceration in infants); hydrocele; communication; processus vaginalis
